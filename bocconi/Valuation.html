1 Restating Financial Statements

1.1 Estimating Cost of Capital

1.2 Requirements

1.2.1 Accounting

  1. ASSETS = LIABILITIES + EQUITY
    1. Operating Assets = Operating Liabilities + LT Debt + Equity
  2. WORKING CAPITAL: The categories related to the cycle of business operations i.e. inventory, accounts receivable, and accounts payable.

1.2.2 Corporate Finance

  1. RETURN = PROFIT/INVESTMENT
  2. FCF = NOPLAT − Net Increase in Invested Capital

1.2.3 Basic Principles

  1. Logic of differentiation between operating/financial parts of company during valuation
  2. Need for reorganization of financial statements

1.3 Calculating Investment(IC = "Investment Capital")

1.3.1 Theory

  1. Invested Capital = LT Debt + Equity = Operating Assets − Operating Liabilities
  2. Total Funds Invested = Invested Capital + Non Operating Assets

1.4 Calculating Return(NOPLAT = "Net Operating Profit Less Adjusted Taxes")

1.4.1 Theory

  1. NOPLAT is operating profit available to both debt/equity holders.

1.4.2 Procedure

  1. First, interest is not subtracted from operating profit, because interest is considered a payment to the company’s financial investors, not an operating expense
  2. Second, exclude any nonoperating income generated from assets that were excluded from invested capital
  3. Thirdly, to calculate operating taxes, start with reported taxes, add back the tax shield caused by interest expense, and remove the taxes paid on nonoperating income.
