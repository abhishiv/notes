1 IN-PROGRESS Restating Financial Statements

1.1 Definitions

1.1.1 Accounting

  1. ASSETS = LIABILITIES + EQUITY
    1. Operating Assets = Operating Liabilities + LT Debt + Equity
  2. WORKING CAPITAL: The categories related to the cycle of business operations i.e. inventory, accounts receivable, and accounts payable.

1.1.2 Corporate Finance

  1. RETURN = PROFIT/INVESTMENT
  2. FCF = NOPLAT − Net Increase in Invested Capital

1.1.3 Basic Principles

  1. Logic of differentiation between operating/financial parts of company during valuation
  2. Need for reorganization of financial statements

1.2 Calculating Investment(IC = "Investment Capital")

1.2.1 Theory

  1. Invested Capital = LT Debt + Equity = Operating Assets − Operating Liabilities
  2. Total Funds Invested = Invested Capital + Non Operating Assets

1.3 Calculating Return(NOPLAT = "Net Operating Profit Less Adjusted Taxes")

1.3.1 Theory

  1. NOPLAT is operating profit available to both debt/equity holders.

1.3.2 Procedure

  1. First, interest is not subtracted from operating profit, because interest is considered a payment to the company’s financial investors, not an operating expense
  2. Second, exclude any nonoperating income generated from assets that were excluded from invested capital
  3. Thirdly, to calculate operating taxes, start with reported taxes, add back the tax shield caused by interest expense, and remove the taxes paid on nonoperating income.

2 TODO Estimating Cost of Capital

2.1 Definitions

2.1.1 Keu: unlevered cost of equity

Rate of return acceptable by equity investors in a completely unlevered firm. Reflects the risk profile associated with corporate assets ignoring capital structure.

2.1.2 Kd: levered cost of equity

Rate of return acceptable by a form debt.

2.1.3 WACC = (D/V)*Kd*(1 - T) + (E/V)*ke

2.2 Estimated unlevered cost of equity

2.2.1 CAPM

Ri = rf + Bi[Rm - rf] Here, Beta represents a stock’s incremental risk to a diversified investor, where risk is defined as the extent to which the stock covaries with the aggregate stock market. High beta means company must earn and offer higher return to entice investors.

  1. Estimating Risk free rate rf: use zero coupon treasury bonds
  2. Estimating Market risk premium: 5-6%
  3. Estimating Beta

    Use industry beta, instead of company beta. To undo/normalise the effect of leverage use Modiligani and Miller.

2.3 Estimating cost of debt

After-Tax Cost of Debt = Cost of Debt × (1 − Tm)

5 IN-PROGRESS Multiples

The multiples method seeks to develop a relationship between the actual price of shares of comparable listed companies and an accounting metric (such as net income, cash flows, revenues, etc.)

Enterprise Value Equity Value
EV/EBIT Price by Earning: P/E
EV/EBITDA Price by Cash earning: P/CE
EV/Sales Price by Book Value: P/BV

5.1 Theory

5.1.1 Assumptions

  1. The company’s value changes proportionally to the internal variables chosen as a measure of performance;
  2. The growth rates of both cash flows and the risk level are constant

5.1.2 Concept

  • The financial method creates cash flow projections based on estimations on cost of capital, and projection on growth rate.
  • The multiples method avoids these estimations and takes the expected growth rate and risk appreciation directly from market data through the use of multiples.
  1. Stock and Deal Multiples

    Multiples can refer to values taken from two different market contexts:

    • The stock market
    • The market for corporate control

    MARKET VALUE = STAND ALONE VALUE + PREMIUM

  2. Financial and Business Multiples
    1. Financial multiples identify the connection between a equity market price and some important measures such as cash flows, net income, or EBITDA
    2. Business multiples link the equity market price to specific elements which are relevant for the business model typical of the sector the company(RPU for telecom i.e.)

5.1.3 Common multiples

  1. P or EV

    P can be calculated by EV by subtracting market value of debt.

  2. Direct or Indirect
    • Direct: Measure how much value company is generating P/E, or EV/EBITDA
    • Indirect: Unable to measure this - P/BV, or EV/Sales
  3. Current, Trailing, and Leading Multiples
    • Current: last available balance sheet
    • Trailing: last 12m
    • Leading: future results
    Current Trailing Leading
    P0/Et0 P0/Eltm P0/Et1

    We should be consistet in which time period to choose in calculated, and then applying that multiple to variables.
